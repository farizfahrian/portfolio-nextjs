# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

The primary audience is recruiters evaluating Fariz Fahrian for software engineering and product-minded frontend roles. The secondary audience is freelance or agency clients looking for someone who can design, build, and reason through real digital systems. Collaborators are a tertiary audience, mainly looking for technical taste, reliability, and clear working style.

Visitors usually arrive with limited time and need to understand capability quickly: what Fariz builds, how he thinks, which technologies he uses, and whether his project work shows enough depth to trust him with production responsibilities.

## Product Purpose

This portfolio exists to present Fariz as a Software Engineer and UI/UX Designer who combines clean implementation, thoughtful interface design, and practical business context. Success means a recruiter, client, or collaborator can quickly see credible engineering skill, understand the reasoning behind the work, and feel confident starting a conversation.

The site should do more than list tools. It should show evidence: strong case studies, technical decisions, system constraints, product outcomes, and the ability to turn ambiguous needs into working software.

## Brand Personality

Technical, thoughtful, polished.

The brand should feel like a clean engineering portfolio with premium product design taste. It should communicate calm competence: precise enough for engineering evaluators, refined enough for design-sensitive clients, and practical enough to show that the work ships in real contexts.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic dark developer portfolios that rely on terminal aesthetics without substance.

Avoid crowded resume pages that compress every credential into dense, low-hierarchy blocks.

Avoid trendy SaaS landing page patterns that look polished but lack engineering depth, case-study substance, or personal point of view.

Avoid gimmicky motion, decorative complexity, and visual treatments that make the portfolio harder to read or navigate.

## Design Principles

Show reasoning, not just output. Project sections should reveal what was built, why decisions were made, and how constraints were handled.

Lead with trust signals. Recruiters and clients should quickly see real project experience, ownership, technical stack, and credible outcomes.

Balance engineering clarity with product design taste. The interface should feel organized, calm, and premium without becoming sterile or generic.

Keep hierarchy decisive. Each section needs a clear job, strong scanning structure, and enough whitespace for the work to feel deliberate.

Use motion as polish, not proof. Animation may add refinement, but content, structure, and usability carry the brand.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

The portfolio should meet a practical WCAG AA accessibility baseline. Text contrast should be strong, font sizes should remain readable across devices, and focus states should be clear for keyboard users.

Interactions should be keyboard navigable, buttons and links should use accessible semantics, meaningful images should include useful alt text, and page structure should use semantic HTML.

Animations are allowed when subtle and non-blocking. Motion should respect `prefers-reduced-motion` by reducing or disabling nonessential animation while preserving the same content and functionality.
